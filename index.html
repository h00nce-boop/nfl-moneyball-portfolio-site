Public-data screen for NFL roster value

Who deserves a closer look before his price catches up?

This project turns public NFL production, contract-cost proxies, draft context, and opportunity data into a simple review queue for offensive skill players.

In plain English: the model does not say “sign this player.” It says “this player produced better than his public cost profile, and similar historical players were more likely to stay relevant next season.”

How to use the tool

A non-technical team user should not have to guess what to click. This is the intended workflow.

Three-click workflow

  1. Find Players: build a board by season, team, position, contract stage, draft capital, confidence, and value gap.
  2. Explain one row: read why the model flagged that player-season.
  3. Validate the Signal: check whether the profile historically beat similar non-candidates.
What a flagged row means

“This player is worth review.” A team would still check film, medical history, scheme fit, depth chart, age curve, and internal cap accounting.

What value means here

The model is built around ranks because rank gaps are easy to explain to non-technical readers.

Player value gap
player_value_gap = cost_rank_within_position - production_rank_within_position

A positive value gap means the player produced better than his public cost rank would imply.

Production score
production_score = EPA-based production + lightweight box-score context

EPA is the core production input, with supporting terms for yards, touchdowns, receptions, interceptions, and sacks depending on position.

Glossary for normal people

These labels should appear near the visuals and inside the dashboard, because nobody should have to decode column names.

Value GapHow far a player’s production rank beat his public cost rank. Positive is good.
EPAExpected Points Added. A football value stat that credits plays based on game situation, not just raw yards.
Public Cap CostA public-data contract-cost proxy. Useful for screening, but not the same as a club’s internal cap accounting.
Matched ControlsSimilar players who were not flagged by the model. They make the validation comparison fairer than comparing against everyone.
LiftThe difference between the flagged group’s rate and the matched-control rate. Example: 56% minus 42% equals +14 percentage points.

What this can and cannot claim

Supported

Safe claims

  • This player produced more than his public cost rank suggested.
  • This profile showed positive next-season lift versus matched peers.
  • This row is worth deeper scouting and cap review.
Unsupported

Claims to avoid

  • This proves the player is objectively underpaid.
  • This guarantees a future breakout.
  • This replaces scouting, medical, scheme, or internal team cap data.
EPA limitation

EPA is useful because it accounts for down, distance, field position, and scoring context. It is limited for individual valuation because it can blend player skill with scheme, quarterback play, offensive line quality, teammate quality, opponent strength, role, and coaching.
